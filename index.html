Current Project

Beyond "Palabras": Signaling Meaning through Paratextual Features of Hispanic Cyberfeminism

This research analyzes a corpus of 3.5 million Spanish tweets from 2009 to 2022. By moving beyond text-centric methods, the study explores how paratextual features—such as punctuation, capitalization, and emoji usage—serve as strategic signal selections to convey identity and intensity within the #Feminismo movement.

Keywords: Hispanic Cyberfeminism, NLP, Paratextual Features, Social Media, #Feminismo

Research Interests

  • Spanish as a 21st-century Language
  • Computational Linguistics & NLP
